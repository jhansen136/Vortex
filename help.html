📞 Phone Calls
Phone calls are reserved for life-safety events — situations where a push notification might be missed. All calls play an alert tone followed by a voice message identifying the warning type and your location.
🌪 Tornado Warning Call CALLPRO

Triggered when the National Weather Service issues a Tornado Warning that includes your home location or a pinned city you've enabled alerts for.

You'll receive at most one call per active warning — if the same warning is extended or updated, you won't be called again for it.

🌊 Flash Flood Warning Call CALLPRO

Triggered by a Flash Flood Warning issued for your location. Disabled by default — enable it in Settings under Call Alerts.

📍 Proximity Alert Call CALLPRO

Triggered when a tornado or flood warning's storm is within your configured proximity radius (default 5 miles) and moving toward you.

Unlike the warning call — which is based on your county — the proximity check scans all active tornado and flood warnings within range regardless of county lines. A tornado in the next county 3 miles away and heading your direction will trigger this call.

This may be the first and only call you receive. If the storm is in a neighboring county and your county hasn't been included in the warning, you won't get a warning call — but if the storm is within range and approaching, the proximity alert call will still fire.

Two conditions must both pass before the call fires:

1. Distance — the storm (using NWS GPS coordinates when available, warning polygon edge as fallback) is within your proximity radius.
2. Direction — the storm's heading is within 90° of the bearing toward you. Storms moving away or parallel are suppressed.

When GPS data is available: "The tornado is 3 miles from your home and approaching."
Daily call cap: 5 calls per day. If the cap is reached, VORTEX will send a push notification in place of any additional calls for the rest of that calendar day. The cap resets at midnight.
🔔 Push Notifications
Delivered via ntfy.sh. Requires the ntfy app installed on your phone and your channel URL configured in Settings → Integrations. Each alert type can be toggled on or off individually.
⚠️ NWS Warning Alerts PUSHPRO

Sent when the National Weather Service issues any of the following for your location:

🌪 Tornado Warning  ·  👁 Tornado Watch
⛈ Severe Thunderstorm Warning
🌊 Flash Flood Warning  ·  💧 Flood Warning
❄️ Winter Storm / Blizzard / Ice Storm Warning
💨 High Wind / Extreme Wind Warning

Pushes are deduplicated — the same active alert won't push more than once.

📊 Risk Score Alert PUSHPRO

Sent when your atmospheric Risk Score exceeds your configured threshold, even when no NWS warning is active. This is your early signal that dangerous conditions are developing at your specific location — before an official warning is issued.

Push only — no call. 30-minute cooldown per trigger. Your threshold can be set between 65–100 in Settings.

📉 Pressure Drop Alert PUSHCOMING SOON

Sent when barometric pressure drops rapidly at your location — a real-time signal that a severe storm is actively approaching. Triggers when pressure falls ≥ 2 mb in a 30-minute window. 30-minute cooldown.

⚡ The Risk Score

The Risk Score (0–100) is VORTEX's composite index of how dangerous current atmospheric conditions are at your location. It's calculated from live forecast data and updated every minute by the background engine.

📍 Alert Locations

All alerts apply to your home location by default. You can also enable alerts for any pinned city by toggling the Alert switch on that city in Settings → My Cities.

The background engine checks all alert-enabled locations on every run — home location and any alert-enabled cities are all monitored simultaneously.
