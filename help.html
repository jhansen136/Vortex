📱 Getting Started
Install on iPhone

VORTEX is a Progressive Web App — no App Store required. To install it on your iPhone home screen:

1. Open vortexintel.app in Safari
2. Tap the Share button (box with arrow)
3. Tap "Add to Home Screen"

Once installed, VORTEX runs full screen like a native app.

Install on Android

1. Open vortexintel.app in Chrome
2. Tap the three-dot menu
3. Tap "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App"

Set Your Home Location

Go to Settings → Home Location and tap the map or search for your address. Your home location is used for all background alerting — this is the most important setup step.

📞 Phone Calls
Phone calls are reserved for life-safety events — situations where a push notification might be missed. All calls play an alert tone followed by a voice message identifying the warning type and your location.
🌪 Tornado Warning Call CALLPRO

Triggered when the National Weather Service issues a Tornado Warning that includes your home location or a pinned city you've enabled alerts for.

You'll receive at most one call per active warning — if the same warning is extended or updated, you won't be called again for it.

🌊 Flash Flood Warning Call CALLPRO

Triggered by a Flash Flood Warning issued for your location. Disabled by default — enable it in Settings under Call Alerts.

📍 Proximity Alert Call CALLPRO

Triggered when a tornado warning's storm is within your configured proximity radius (default 5 miles) and moving toward you.

Unlike the warning call — which is based on your county — the proximity check scans all active tornado warnings within range regardless of county lines. A tornado in the next county 3 miles away and heading your direction will trigger this call.

This may be the first and only call you receive. If the storm is in a neighboring county and your county hasn't been included in the warning, you won't get a warning call — but if the storm is within range and approaching, the proximity alert call will still fire.

Two conditions must both pass before the call fires:

1. Distance — the storm (using NWS GPS coordinates when available, warning polygon edge as fallback) is within your proximity radius.
2. Direction — the storm's heading is within 90° of the bearing toward you. Storms moving away or parallel are suppressed.

When GPS data is available: "The tornado is 3 miles from your home and approaching."
Daily call cap: 5 calls per day. If the cap is reached, VORTEX will send a push notification in place of any additional calls for the rest of that calendar day. The cap resets at midnight.
📲 What Number Will Call Me?

All VORTEX alert calls come from (479) 552-5858.

Save this number in your contacts as VORTEX Alert so you recognize it immediately — and so Emergency Bypass works correctly (see below).

📵 Making Sure Calls Ring Through Do Not Disturb

VORTEX places one call and reads the alert message twice within that call — so if you pick up partway through, you hear the full message. For the most reliable alerting, set up Emergency Bypass on the VORTEX contact directly.

iPhone — Emergency Bypass (Recommended)

Emergency Bypass allows a specific contact to ring and vibrate even when Do Not Disturb or Silent mode is on:

1. Save the VORTEX alert number as a contact (e.g. VORTEX Alert)
2. Open the contact → tap Edit
3. Tap Ringtone → enable Emergency Bypass
4. Tap Vibration → enable Emergency Bypass there too

With both enabled, the call will ring and vibrate regardless of Do Not Disturb, Silent mode, or Focus settings.

Lock Your Ring Volume (Optional)

iOS allows apps and buttons to change your ringer volume independently of your media volume. To prevent your ring volume from being turned down accidentally:

Go to Settings → Sounds & Haptics → disable "Change with Buttons". This locks your ringer volume at its current level — note that this affects all incoming calls, not just VORTEX.

Emergency Bypass is per-contact and does not affect your ringer volume or other calls. Locking ringer volume is phone-wide. We recommend Emergency Bypass as the primary setup step.
🔔 Push Notifications
Delivered via ntfy.sh. Requires the ntfy app installed on your phone and your channel URL configured in Settings → Integrations. Each alert type can be toggled on or off individually.
⚠️ NWS Warning Alerts PUSHPRO

Sent when the National Weather Service issues any of the following for your location:

🌪 Tornado Warning  ·  👁 Tornado Watch
⛈ Severe Thunderstorm Warning
🌊 Flash Flood Warning  ·  💧 Flood Warning
❄️ Winter Storm / Blizzard / Ice Storm Warning
💨 High Wind / Extreme Wind Warning

Pushes are deduplicated — the same active alert won't push more than once.

📊 Risk Score Alert PUSHPRO

Sent when your atmospheric Risk Score exceeds your configured threshold, even when no NWS warning is active. This is your early signal that dangerous conditions are developing at your specific location — before an official warning is issued.

Push only — no call. 30-minute cooldown per trigger. Your threshold can be set between 65–100 in Settings.

📉 Pressure Drop Alert PUSHPRO

Sent when barometric pressure drops rapidly at your location — a real-time signal that a severe storm is actively approaching. Triggers when pressure falls ≥ 2 mb in a 30-minute window. 30-minute cooldown.

🔔 ntfy Setup

VORTEX delivers push notifications via ntfy.sh — a free, open-source notification service. You control your own private channel. No account required.

Step 1 — Install the ntfy App

Download the ntfy app from the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play (Android). Search for ntfy.

Step 2 — Create a Private Channel

In the ntfy app, tap + Subscribe to topic and enter a unique name — something only you would know, like vortex-yourname-8472. This becomes your private channel.

Anyone who knows your channel name can send you notifications — keep it private and unpredictable.
Step 3 — Add Your Channel URL to VORTEX

In VORTEX, go to Settings → Integrations → ntfy Channel URL and enter:

https://ntfy.sh/your-channel-name

That's it. VORTEX will send all push alerts to that channel from now on.

Allow Notifications on iPhone

On iPhone, go to Settings → ntfy → Notifications and make sure notifications are allowed. For critical alerts to break through Do Not Disturb, enable Critical Alerts if prompted.

📍 Alert Locations

All alerts apply to your home location by default. You can also enable alerts for any pinned city by toggling the Alert switch on that city in Settings → My Cities.

The background engine checks all alert-enabled locations on every run — home location and any alert-enabled cities are all monitored simultaneously.

Pro accounts can have up to 3 alert-enabled cities in addition to the home location. You can pin unlimited cities to the map, but only 3 can have background alerting active at a time. Disable one to free up a slot.
